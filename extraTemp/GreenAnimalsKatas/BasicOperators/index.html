Basic Operators

Terminology Operators are unary, binary, or ternary: Unary operators operate on a single target (such as -a). Unary prefix operators appear immediately before their target (such as !b), and unary postfix operators appear immediately after their target (such as c!). inary operators operate on two targets (such as 2 + 3) and are infix because they appear in between their two targets. Ternary operators operate on three targets. Like C, Swift has only one ternary operator, the ternary conditional operator (a ? b : c).
